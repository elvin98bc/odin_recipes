Recipe 1: Nasi Lemak

Description

Nasi lemak is a Malaysian culinary delight, featuring fragrant coconut rice served with anchovies, peanuts, boiled eggs, cucumber, and a kick of spicy sambal. This iconic dish encapsulates the perfect blend of savory and aromatic flavors, making it a beloved breakfast or anytime treat.

Ingredient

  • 1/2 cup Fragrant rice
  • 1/4 cup Coconut milk
  • 1 Pandan leaf (tied into a knot)
  • 1/4 teaspoon Salt
  • 2 tablespoons Dried anchovies
  • 1 tablespoon Peanuts
  • 1-2 Boiled eggs
  • A few slices of Cucumber
  • 2 tablespoons Sambal (adjust to taste)
  • Sliced shallots (optional for garnish)
  • Sliced cucumber (optional for garnish)
  • Fried chicken or fish (optional for added protein)

Steps

  1. Wash and rinse 1/2 cup of Fragrant rice.
  2. In a pot, combine the rice, 1/4 cup of Coconut milk, a tied Pandan leaf, and 1/4 teaspoon of Salt. Cook the rice until done.
  3. While the rice is cooking, prepare 2 tablespoons of Dried anchovies and 1 tablespoon of Peanuts by roasting them until fragrant.
  4. Boil 1-2 Eggs until they are cooked to your liking.
  5. Slice a cucumber into a few thin slices.
  6. Once the rice is ready, fluff it with a fork and remove the Pandan leaf.
  7. Assemble the Nasi Lemak by placing a portion of the coconut rice on a plate. Top it with the roasted anchovies, peanuts, boiled eggs, and sliced cucumber.
  8. Serve with 2 tablespoons of Sambal on the side (adjust to taste).
  9. Garnish with sliced shallots and additional cucumber if desired.
  10. Optionally, include Fried chicken or fish for added protein.
